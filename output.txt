Dralha Flour Mill</strong></p><p><strong>Established:</strong> 1988<br /><strong>Location:</strong> Industrial Estate, Phuentsholing, Bhutan<br />Email: <a href="mailto:dralhaflourmill@gmail.com">dralhaflourmill@gmail.com</a> </p><p>Dralha Flour Mill, established in 1988, stands as the pioneering flour mill in Bhutan, setting an exemplary standard within the country’s flour production industry. As the only flour mill operating in the Kingdom, the company has played a critical role in fulfilling domestic demand for high-quality flour products.</p><p>Located in the Phuentsholing Industrial Estate, the facility initially operated with an installed production capacity of 60 tons per day (TPD). In 2010, the mill underwent a major modernization and capacity expansion, increasing its production capability to 100 TPD.</p><p>The milling process at Dralha Flour Mill begins with the meticulous cleaning and tempering of wheat kernels, followed by controlled grinding using specialized roller systems that split the kernels open. The resulting particles, known as break flours, are then carefully sieved and processed before final packaging.</p><p>The company’s core products include:</p><ul><li><strong>Atta (Whole Wheat Flour)</strong></li><li><strong>Maida (Refined Flour)</strong></li><li><strong>Suji (Semolina)</strong></li><li><strong>Wheat Bran</strong></li></ul><p>These products have gained strong market acceptance due to their competitive pricing, consistent quality, and reliability. Raw materials are sourced from key agricultural regions in Bihar and West Bengal in India, as well as through procurement partnerships with the National Cooperative Export Ltd in New Delhi.</p><p>In addition to serving the domestic Bhutanese market, Dralha Flour Mill exports products to India and Bangladesh, strengthening the company’s regional trade presence.</p><p><strong>Future Plans</strong></p><p>To further diversify and expand its operations, Dralha Flour Mill plans to introduce several value-added food manufacturing initiatives, including:</p><ul><li>Expansion into <strong>wheat-based cookies and biscuits</strong></li><li>Production of <strong>healthy cereal products</strong></li><li>Establishment of a <strong>bread manufacturing unit</strong></li><li>Increase the overall production capacity </li><li>Development of a <strong>noodle production facility</strong></li></ul><p>Dralha Flour Mill has built significant goodwill both domestically and internationally through its commitment to food safety, quality control, and consistent supply. As Bhutan’s sole flour milling operation, the company remains dedicated to maintaining and enhancing product quality while meeting the country’s growing demand.</p><p>A key competitive advantage for Dralha Flour Mill lies in Bhutan’s low electricity tariffs, which are among the most competitive in South Asia, allowing the company to maintain cost efficiency and strengthen its position in export markets.</p><p><strong>Factory Relocation</strong></p><p>Effective June 2026, Dralha Flour Mill has commenced relocating its entire factory operations from Phuentsholing to the new Industrial Estate in Dam Dum, Samtse, located in southwestern Bhutan.
